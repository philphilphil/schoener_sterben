# Opera Blog Naming Report — Gist

## Blog Context

A German-language opera blog by a non-expert passionate listener. Makes opera accessible to younger, culturally interested audiences. Uses pop culture hooks (Kill Bill, Counter Strike, pizza) as entry points. Tone: warm, personal, inviting — "come along, this is actually cool." Previous name "Schöner Sterben" was clever but too dark/provocative for the blog's actual vibe.

## Seven Naming Mechanisms

### 1. Musical terminology as everyday metaphor
Use terms like Zugabe, Ouvertüre, Crescendo, Zwischenspiel, Da Capo — words that carry both precise musical meaning and natural metaphorical resonance. Choose terms where the metaphorical meaning aligns with the blog's mission.

### 2. German compound-word collisions
Fuse high culture into everyday life in a single compound: Opernluft, Pausenbrot, Opernküche. These carry built-in narrative tension.

### 3. Oxymoronic pairing
Place a traditional concept next to a subversive modifier: Klassik drastisch, Klassikpunk. Signals credibility AND irreverence.

### 4. Idiomatic substitution
Take a well-known German phrase and swap one element. "Quatsch keine Opern!" reclaims "don't talk nonsense." "Die Entführung aus dem Alltag" transforms Mozart into a mission statement.

### 5. Register code-switching
Mix English casualness with German cultural weight (HOW TO OPER), or Italian musical vocabulary with German everyday language.

### 6. Appetite and physicality metaphors
Operavore, Opernsüchtig, Opernfieber — reframe opera as physical craving rather than intellectual exercise.

### 7. Punctuation as tone
OPER! transforms a neutral noun into an exclamation. Zugabe! carries its own built-in energy.

## Best-Fit Directions for This Blog

- **Pause/intermission words**: The intermission is when you turn to a friend and say "wasn't that amazing?" — which is what this blog does. (Pausenplausch, Pausengespräch, Pausenbrot)
- **Zugabe** (encore): Inherently enthusiastic, short, punchy.
- **Opernluft**: Freshness, open windows — antidote to opera's stuffy reputation.
- **Ganz Ohr**: Common idiom for open curiosity — perfect non-expert listener posture.
- **Wagner-derived names**: Waldweben, Vorabend, Wanderer, Waldvogel — poetic, work with or without Ring knowledge.

## What Makes a Name Stick

1. Works on two frequencies — one for insiders, one for newcomers
2. Communicates editorial posture in a single breath
3. Short — ideally under three words
4. Leads with curiosity, warmth, or appetite — not provocation
